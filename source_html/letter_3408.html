My dear Mary Yonge I am glad you have been keeping the wedding day with Charlotte Cordelia (Pode) Yonge.[[footnote:1]] I am afraid that the hotel must spoil sitting out of doors except in front of the house. I am writing now in the garden while Blanche C. Webber, who is here to recover from the remains of the influenza is lying down in her own room. She had it at Easter and does not quite get over the remains, so she is come to breathe her native air here, till Major Robert Woollcombe RMA and Mary (Walter) Woollcombe come on the 22d. I believe they go to Dorking today. I think it is rather cruel of Baldwin John Pollexfen Bastard to forbid the parties from Plymouth as they do not molest him.[[footnote:2]] I was asked the other day if Kitley was a desirable place for a young laundry maid Girls’ Friendly Society, The. I could not say that I thought I should choose it. I had a nice letter today from one of our Dr. Barnardo's Homes girls who was sent to Canada five years ago. She has always been in the same place, and has had two prizes from her Sunday school at Toronto, where she was pleased to find Yonge Street.[[footnote:3]] She had been on an expedition in a steamer to see Niagara, but they only had ten minutes there – rather American like the man who said enough of Lago Maggiore in one flash of lightning- My budlea[sic] is looking very pretty. No one but Devonshire people ever seem to have seen one. My guests always ask what it is. I kept The Rev. Charles Burell Yonge’s wedding at a garden party at the The Rt. Rev. George Henry Sumner’s[[footnote:4]], where among many other people I met Mrs Spender[[footnote:5]], who was pleased to hear about the wedding. Edward Harold Spender has written an interesting Through the High Pyrenees about the Pyrenees, starting from the Mediterranean, which I never heard about before. I am so glad Charles did not consent to the display of wedding presents, I always dislike the notices so much, but then as The Rev. George Rundle Prynnesaid when his father and mother were married ‘The Yonges are not demonstrative’ Poor old Mr Prynne, he and The Rev. Canon Thomas Thellusson Carter are nearly the only survivors of the old days.[[footnote:6]] Canon Carter still preaches, though he is 90. We have had scattered up and down the village a tract with a regular attack on the The Rt. Rev. Edward White Benson, called ‘The Real Presence’ evidently written by an actual NonConformist. Scraps of it have been picked up along the road so some at least must have been torn up. The Rev. Henry Albany Bowles jr. goes to Switzerland on the 17th of July, and Louisa Alethea (Yonge) Bowles and the children to Holmwood close to Dorking. Joanna Angela (Yonge) Cromie’s two little boys sail with their nurse on the 26th. She has been to a ball at Gibraltar and it will be much pleasanter than Morocco.

Our English Church Union, The festival is to be at Cranbury on the 21th. If the weather holds it will be beautiful but rain is wanted, though not by the hay which is almost got in. Charlotte Cordelia (Pode) Yonge must be enjoying the garden at Slade, which The Rev. Edmund Doidge Anderson Morshead and I agreed was the most beautiful we knew. Edmund and Mary (Fearon) Anderson Morshead called the other day

your most affec C M Yonge
