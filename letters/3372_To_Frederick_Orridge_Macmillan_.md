'

Dear [Mr Macmillan (Frederick Orridge Macmillan)](../people/942.html) I have been so often asked for a cheaper edition of my novels that I am delighted to hear that you are beginning on one of them, and I hope it will answer so well that you may follow it up with others

I hope by the 1st of July to send you the [*'Parishes of John Keble' (John Keble's Parishes: A History of Hursley and Otterbourne. By Charlotte M. Yonge, An Old Inhabitant.)*](../bibliography/cmy/247.html). It is being looked over by the Heathcote family now, and I shall not have it back till the 18th when I hope to put the last touches. A great deal of curious history has come out about the various old houses and the book will be of more general interest than I thought at first. I send another instalment of the [*Cameos (Cameos from English History: IX. The Eighteenth Century. By the author of "The Heir of Redclyffe.")*](../bibliography/cmy/249.html) for the printers

Yours sincerely C M Yonge
