'

My dear [C C (Christabel Rose Coleridge)](../people/329.html) I am glad there are some signs of life or of decay more properly of poor [A D I (Arthur Donald Innes)](../people/744.html)- ! I do think there are more and more signs that a 6d phoenix would be welcomed.[^1]

I find the booksellers make all sorts of excuses for not having got it -- The [Authors Soc (Society of Authors, The)](../organizations/44.html) want to see my agreement before advising me. I could not find it easily before I left home and was too hot for a hunt. [FMP (Frances Mary Peard)](../people/1160.html) has written to offer a visit on the 17th or 18th so I have written to her to meet me and come with me on the 18th I have got the MS of [*Ducks Eggs (Modern Broods, or, Developments Unlooked For. )*](../bibliography/cmy/251.html), I think I can make them much more interesting and longer. We are nice and cool here now, but I think people must suppose that I dispose of all the [scholarship (Charlotte Yonge Scholarship, The)](../organizations/113.html) money for the begging letters are endless. [Anna Bramston (Anna Rachel Bramston)](../people/195.html) has photographed all the tableaux before the break up! Poor [George (George Alan \[or Allan?\] David Yonge)](../people/1610.html) at Kimberley is tormented as nephew to a lion, and was invited to go to a book dance as '[*The Daisy Chain (Daisy Chain, or, Aspirations: A Family Chronicle, The. By the author of "The Heir of Redclyffe." 2 Vols)*](../bibliography/cmy/24.html)'- pretty well for a great black police officer. So you may tell [Walter (Walter Henry Praed Coleridge)](../people/353.html) to prepare to be invited out as Jack a Lantern. [Helen (Helen Emma Yonge)](../people/1613.html) returns remembrances. I have had a charming letter from the old [Mignonette (Mary Frances Younghusband)](../people/1637.html) about Maximilian and the Martinswand- and an Italian palace -- quite good enough for an article. [^1] The local demon [Mr ffinch (M.M. ffinch)](../people/1688.html) & co are a great success, he reads the lessons so beautifully that I am quite sorry to miss a fortnight of him. He is warden of a college for 'decayed gentlewomen' I should think you would have heard of it.

[Maurice (Maurice Edward Yonge)](../people/1630.html) and [Maude (Maude Yonge)](../people/1629.html) have the funniest little cottage at Fleet, in the middle of purple heath

your affectionate C M Yonge

Poor Lanty!

---

[^1]: They were hoping that the <i>[[other:52]MP]</i> would be revived in a cheaper form.
[^2]: The legend that the emperor Maximilian I (1459-1519) climbed the Martinswand near Innsbruck is referred to in CMY’s novel <i>*The Dove in the Eagle’s Nest*</i> (1866) and is also the subject of one of Schubert’s songs.
