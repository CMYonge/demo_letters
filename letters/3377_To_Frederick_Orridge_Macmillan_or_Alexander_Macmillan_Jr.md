'

Dear [Mr Macmillan (Frederick Orridge Macmillan)](../people/942.html) I hope to send off the illustrations - or the material for them on Saturday.[^2]

I will write to [Mr Portal (Melville Portal)](../people/1196.html), the Chairman of the Quarter Sessions for permission for the portrait of [Sir Wm Heathcote (William Heathcote)](../people/669.html)

Two books are with them - one ([Lady Heathcote (Selina Heathcote)](../people/665.html)s) for the sake of the old views and plans - it is the original [*book by Marsh (Memoranda of the Parishes of Hursley and North Baddesley, in the County of Southampton)*](../bibliography/general/734.html).

The [*other (A Guide to the Village of Hursley, the Home of Keble, author of 'The Christian Year')*](../bibliography/general/124.html) belonging to me is the [Revd J.F. Moor (John Frewen Moor)](../people/1046.html)'s written about 1860.

The photographs are by a man named [Savage (William Savage)](../people/1283.html), who is dead I marked what are worth looking at also two little woodcuts The photo of the Hursley Vicarage is much better than that in the envelope, and more characteristic, but Frith's may be better than either

I need hardly say that all the collection is valuable and we trust to their return in safety[^3]

Yours truly C M Yonge

Some are sent to be chosen from

---

[^1]: There is a pencil list on the reverse, in another hand: '1. Sir W. Heathcote/ 2. Richard Cromwell/ 3. Keble/ 4. Hursley Church Vicarage (Frith)/ 5. Otterbourne Church	Exterior - Salmon/ Interior  - Oakley/ 6. Hursley Park House (photo)/ 7. Ampfield Church (photo)/ 8. Merdon Castle & wall .'
[^2]: The illustrations relate to *<i>John Keble's Parishes</i>*.
[^3]: A 'List of Illustrations Sent' is MS British Library  Add 54921: 244.
