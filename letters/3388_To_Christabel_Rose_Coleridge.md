'

My dear [C C (Christabel Rose Coleridge)](../people/329.html) Poor dear [Sophy (Sophia Martyn)](../people/974.html), she has been a heavy weight on many minds from the time of [Pena (Mary Sophia Paulina Martyn)](../people/973.html)'s death, in a remarkable way considering the clever, able woman she was.[^1] I heard of her release, for such it was from [Mary Yonge](../people/1626.html) who wanted much, as well as [Charlotte (Charlotte Cordelia Yonge)](../people/1587.html) to come to the funeral, but happily the two witheld each other, in the fogs and the rain and the wet grass, so it was a very good thing they prevented each other-

I am glad [Fanny (Frances Sophia Coleridge Patteson)](../people/1151.html) was there. You have a splendid possession, according to this advt. Mine is only seven volumes. Here is an entertaining letter from Margaret; what storms!

I think SS Agnes and Margaret were rather RC saints for Irish to swallow and I would eliminate Hope Carlyon if possible She seems to me in SPCK to be of a very low order of sense.[^2] The [*Elf ('Elf: The Christmas Number Prize Story')*](../bibliography/general/608.html) is a very beautiful idea, though practically I doubt a birth deformed person being so amiable, I think the temper is generally astray and no wonder. Yes, it is a good number for Christmas. [*La Belle Louise ('La Belle Louise')*](../bibliography/general/606.html) is a charming story, and the [*Household Emergency ('A Household Emergency')*](../bibliography/general/607.html) capital- the others, even [FMP (Frances Mary Peard)](../people/1160.html)'s, very fair commonplace- but it is very good altogether.[^3] Cupid is getting better, but had not been dressed when last we heard of him, it was really a sharp attack, and it was well he had his Granny.

I have written a thing for the G L, on the century and am asked to write one for the [Girls' Realm (*Girls' Realm, The*)](../organizations/210.html)

'[*A Girls' Experiment (A Girl's Experiment)*](../bibliography/general/603.html)' [RTS (Religious Tract Society, The)](../organizations/35.html) is [really]{.underline} good for either degree of girl. [*The Whipping boy (The Whipping-Boy)*](../bibliography/general/604.html) Wells Gardner is also good. [Mary Bramston (Mary Eliza Bramston)](../people/198.html) thinks it possible for Elementary [RU (Girls' Friendly Society Reading Union)](../organizations/211.html)

your affte C M Yonge

---

[^1]: Sophia (Coleridge) Martyn (d. 1898) was the mother of Paulina Martyn ‘Pena’ (1843-1887), one of the founder Goslings.  She was a first cousin of Mary Coleridge and Fanny Patteson.
[^2]: This sentence evidently refers to a review article, perhaps 'Book Notes' <i>MP</i> (December 1898) 715-6, which mentions approvingly <i>The Whipping-Boy</i>.  Hope Carlyon was the author of a number of works including <i>*The Great Storm: A Tale for Mother’s Meetings*</i> (London, SPCK 1897).
[^3]: <i>MP</i> (Christmas 1898) included the 'Elf: The Christmas Number Prize Story'; an anonymous story called 'La Belle Louise'; Nora Kilnorth, 'A Household Emergency'; and Frances Mary Peard, 'The Man in India'.
