'

My dear [C C (Christabel Rose Coleridge)](../people/329.html) Here is a behind the times letter that concerns you. I have heard from [Mr Thring (George Herbert Thring)](../people/1281.html), who says I can remove my books with proper notice, but as I don't know how long proper notice is, I have written to ask, also he thinks there may be trouble about those of which [A D I (Arthur Donald Innes)](../people/744.html) gives Royalty and on the older ones there is no agreement.[^1] I asked if I ought to wait to write to [Macmillan (Macmillan and Son)](../organizations/111.html) till there is some decision about A D I

I don't think, on considering it, that [FMP (Frances Mary Peard)](../people/1160.html)'s opinion goes for much, she has gone too much out of the range of English life. I doubt [Skeffington](../organizations/110.html) since the father died, he has been very ritualistic and also silly and sensational and I should question if he had extensive connection enough. [Darton](../organizations/109.html), if he has a soul for it would be better, and I am sure it is safer to be a mere publisher of principle and good taste and not an aspirant author which was one mistake.

Yes from 18 to 30 is the age to write for, but with a certain view to what might be good or bad for the younger ones who would read also. [FMP (Frances Mary Peard)](../people/1160.html) has been staying in Scotland and with [Lady Macclesfield (Mary Parker)](../people/1655.html), also [Miss Acland (Sarah Angelina Acland)](../people/8.html) /Oxford\\\\, so she has been seeing varieties. I shall have [Annie Moberly (Charlotte Anne Elizabeth Moberly)](../people/1024.html) for a week in September and she is of much use to talk to, though too busy to be any good when her term begins. I did not like to ask FMP point blank, but I doubt her having read [*the Long Vacation (Long Vacation, The)*](../bibliography/cmy/237.html) where I think Emilia, Gillian and Dolores are all essentially modern young ladies. [^2]

The glass is really going down but there is hardly a blade of grass left except under the trees and in the water meadows. Yarrow and plantain, also thistles do what they can

your affectionate C M Yonge

---

[^1]: George Herbert Thring (1859-1941), solicitor, Secretary of the [[other:44]Society of Authors], 1891-1906.  He was the son of Edward Thring, the headmaster of Uppingham whose book on teaching CMY so admired.
