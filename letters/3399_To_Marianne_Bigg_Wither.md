'\[To [Marianne Bigg Wither](../people/1546.html) about the death of her brother the Rev. [William Bigg Wither (William Harris Walter Bigg Wither)](../people/1544.html).\]

I had not heard for a fortnight, and had just made up my mind to write to ask [Raby](../people/1221.html) whether you knew anything, and when I saw your writing I knew how it must be. This gradual, gentle sinking is the most merciful way of going one can think of, though I hope that there may not be the restlessness that belongs to weakness and is so very distressing. I shall ask [Henry Bowles (Henry Albany Bowles)](../people/184.html) to pray for him, especially on Sunday when the people are there. I hope it is peaceful sleepiness, and that [your niece (Florence Gertrude Norsworthy)](../people/1089.html) or one of the nephews can be with him. The last letter I had, now more than a fortnight ago, spoke of going to church at eleven and preaching from his chair.
