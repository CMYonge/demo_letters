'

My dear [Mrs Packe (Penelope Anne Laura Packe)](../people/1112.html) I am sure that the correspondence would be most interesting and suggestive, only I am not quite sure in what way you thought of letting it appear, whether as the actual letters abridged or as a digest of them, or to shew a selection to anyone who might be writing an account of the times. [^2] They would reflect the feeling of the early times surprisingly to many, and I think there would be surprise at how much the plain long services now so much despised were to people like them.[^3]

Perhaps if you liked to talk this over, you would let me know when you are coming to Crookham. I should be very glad if you could spend a day or two here, as I now have room

Yours sincerely C M Yonge

---

[^1]: With envelope addressed to Mrs Packe/Honies House/West Wickham/ Kent and postmarked Winchester 18 April 1899.
[^2]: The lifelong correspondence, now preserved in Hampshire Record Office, between [[person:1379]Anne Sturges Bourne] (who had brought Penelope Packe up) and [[person:464] Mary Anne Dyson].
[^3]: The ‘early times’ are those of the Tractarian revival.
