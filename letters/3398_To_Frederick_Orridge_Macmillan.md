'

Dear [Mr Macmillan (Frederick Orridge Macmillan)](../people/942.html) I believe I wrote before and asked whether you thought it worth while to revive two or three books of mine that have gone out of print. I have been asked about them again, which leads me to write. '[*The Two Guardians (Two Guardians; or, Home in this World, The By the author of "Henrietta's Wish," "Kenneth," etc.)*](../bibliography/cmy/13.html)' and '[*Henrietta's Wish' (Henrietta's Wish, or, Domineering: A Tale. By the author of "Scenes and Characters". )*](../bibliography/cmy/11.html) were published by [Masters](../organizations/112.html) and went through three editions at least, then I heard no more of them and his business has been given up. The copyright is mine.

They are both 'stories for young people' and were a good deal liked and honestly I think there is good work in them.

[*Kenneth or the Rear Guard of the Grand Army (Kenneth, or, The Rear Guard of the Grand Army. By the author of "Scenes and Characters," "Kings of England," etc.)*](../bibliography/cmy/10.html) was published by [Parker of Oxford (John Henry Parker)](../people/1142.html). I believe he has let it drop, but there must be some merit in it since Mr E Arnold wrote to ask permission to make extracts in a Reading book

I thank you much for the beautiful series on Natural History. The birds delight me much

Yours very truly C M Yonge
