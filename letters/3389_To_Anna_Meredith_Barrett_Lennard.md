'

My dear [Mrs Lennard (Anna Meredith Barrett Lennard)](../people/121.html) I suppose your friends are only prepared to make remuneration by entertaining the English folks in return Otherwise I might be able to tell you of someone but everybody is so poor in these days that I am afraid it could hardly be done gratis.

I sent off a magazine by yesterday's post Luckily there was one over as we had had one too many of an extra number and it gives a tolerable notion of the Christmas work. The little bits about the Litany are for helps in the catechising.

Lincolns is turned into a villa and let. I suppose you hear of [Mr Jones Bateman (Rowland Jones-Bateman)](../people/785.html)'s death, a great loss to the parish, and old [Mr Chamberlayne (Thomas Chamberlayne)](../people/282.html) died this spring at 91. His son has not yet come to reside at [Cranbury (Cranbury Park)](../organizations/119.html), where much has to be done to get the place into order.

I am afraid I do not know much about the House of Hohenstaufen before Frederick the Great.

[Lord Blachford (Frederic Rogers)](../people/1252.html)'s [*letters (Letters of Frederic, Lord Blachford, under-secretary of state for the colonies, 1870-1871 edited by George Eden Marindin)*](../bibliography/general/610.html) are as delightful as he was himself, I knew him well and his most beautiful home in Devonshire. You should read [Lord Selborne (Roundell Palmer)](../people/1129.html)'s l[*ife and letters (Memorials)*](../bibliography/general/609.html) which are most striking\[.\] I think his old father's letters are some of the wisest things I ever read

Yours very sincerely C M Yonge

---

[^1]: Envelope addressed to ‘Mrs Barrett Lennard/Dom Vehlow/ In Wultike Priegnitz/ North Brandenburg/ Germany’.
