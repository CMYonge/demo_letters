'

My dear [Lottie (Charlotte Fortescue Yonge)](../people/1588.html) I put off writing till the 19th was over, for it really was a very interesting day, though I little knew beforehand all they were going to make of it. About £1800 was collected for [the scholarship (Charlotte Yonge Scholarship, The)](../organizations/113.html), and this was presented, with a beautifully illuminated address, by [the Bishop (George Henry Sumner)](../people/1382.html) in [the High School (Winchester Girls' High School)](../organizations/51.html), making a wonderful speech about having read the *[*Little Duke (Little Duke, or, Richard the Fearless, The. By the author of "The Heir of Redclyffe", "The Kings of England.")*](../bibliography/cmy/20.html)* when he was a small boy, and all that had turned up about the usefulness of the books. Also they gave me a basket of flowers - [*daisies (Daisy Chain, or, Aspirations: A Family Chronicle, The. By the author of "The Heir of Redclyffe." 2 Vols)*](../bibliography/cmy/24.html), [*heartsease (Heartsease, or, The Brother's Wife. 2 vols. By the author of "The Heir of Redclyffe.")*](../bibliography/cmy/19.html) and the like, with violet ribbons to represent the violet, as of course there were none to be had, and ropes of daisy chain hung all about. Afterwards the girls made some very pretty tableaux from the stories, the *Little Duke*, the *[*Caged Lion (Caged Lion, The By Charlotte M. Yonge, author of "The Heir of Redclyffe".)*](../bibliography/cmy/81.html)*, and the *[*Chaplet of Pearls (Chaplet of Pearls, or, The White and Black Ribaumont, The. By the author of "The Heir of Redclyffe.")*](../bibliography/cmy/69.html)*, and had a daisy-chain dance in thin white frocks. It really was as pretty a sight as ever was; the pity was that I had none of my own people with me, for [Alethea (Louisa Alethea Bowles)](../people/182.html)'s children have all been having the measles, and are not out yet, and [Henry (Henry Albany Bowles)](../people/184.html) is gone to Switzerland to meet his sisters, and have a good bracing holiday. Alley will take the children to lodgings at Dorking or near it as soon as they are safe, and I go to stay with [Frances (Emma Frances Yonge)](../people/1604.html) on the 9th August. She goes in September to her sisters, and then I shall have [Helen (Helen Emma Yonge)](../people/1613.html) for a little while. There is a [Mr. Ffinch (M.M. ffinch)](../people/1688.html) coming for *locum tenens* for the month of August, to lodge at [Miss Finlaison (Marion Finlaison)](../people/512.html)'s.

There has been a bazaar for Chandler's Ford Church, and they made £70, but I am afraid they are still a long way from their church yet. I am sorry you have not a better account of Emma[^1] to give; I hope she will go from home and get a rest. How hot it is! But the beautiful day was a great ingredient in the success of yesterday, as a good deal was out of doors.

I am afraid the poor old *[Monthly Packet (*Monthly Packet, The*)](../organizations/52.html)* is coming to an end, as [Innes (Arthur Donald Innes)](../people/744.html)'s affairs have got into a mess. It has not come out this month, but it may revive at half the price.

Your affectionate cousin C. M. Yonge

---

[^1]: Perhaps her correspondent's sister-in-law, [[person:2081]Emma (Sherlock) Yonge].
