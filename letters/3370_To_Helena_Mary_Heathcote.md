'

My dear [Ellie (Helena Mary Heathcote)](../people/663.html) I am delighted to hear of the Medallion![^1]

Have you seen Sir Herbert Maxwell's [*book of the months (Memories of the Months: Being Pages from the Notebook of a Field-Naturalist and Antiquary)*](../bibliography/general/738.html)-? He disbelieved the rod and someone ought to write to him. He watched [Mullins (John Mullins)](../people/2189.html) - and fancied he had discovered the places before. But [Lady Crawley (Mary Albinia, Lady Crawley-Boevey)](../people/2188.html), the mother of [Mrs William Gibbs (Matilda Blanche Gibbs)](../people/557.html), who had the power only in that generation of the family laughed at it as imagination, and I believe Mrs Gibbs inherited it, but never spoke of it. [Mrs Monroe (Caroline Albinia Monro)](../people/1033.html) had Mullins to find water at Colden Common and he did. All the servants tried under his tuition but only one maid succeeded.

I am doing our Birds with anecdotes if possible to add to the book.

[Helen (Helen Emma Yonge)](../people/1613.html) has a copy of \[illegible\] marked by her [father (Julian Bargus Yonge)](../people/1624.html) and can complete the set. There really is much to say of them.

Your affectionate C M Yonge

---

[^1]: A medallion is mentioned in *<i>John Keble's Parishes</i>*, 3, which is the book under discussion here.
